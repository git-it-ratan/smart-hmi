01 / THE PROBLEM

Traditional HMIs are built for engineers, not operators.

Every major industrial disaster of the past 40 years shares one root cause: operators could not distinguish signal from noise.

ISSUE-01
Alarm Flooding
One pump failure triggers 4–8 downstream alarms shown as completely unrelated events. Operators see chaos, not cause. ISA-18.2 defines anything above 30 alarms/hour as a flood — traditional HMIs routinely hit 600+.
ISSUE-02
No Role Separation
The Operator, Maintenance Engineer, and Supervisor all see the same screen. Three entirely different jobs, one useless data dump. None of them get the specific information they need to act.
ISSUE-03
Code, No Language
ALM-0042: P1_MOTOR_TEMP HIGH — 87.3°C — CRITICAL. What does an operator do with that? Traditional HMIs show raw alarm codes. They do not translate data into action. Operators must carry all context in their heads.
ISSUE-04
Purely Reactive
Traditional HMIs alert only after a threshold is crossed. By then the cascade has already started. There is no early warning based on trend direction. No predictive signal that something is about to fail.

03 / How It Works

Three layers of intelligence.

No black-box ML. A transparent, explainable system that works exactly the way judges can follow in real time.

01
Simulation Engine
10 sensors, 3 failure scenarios, JavaScript timers with realistic propagation delays. A pump trip takes 40 seconds to cascade — exactly like a real plant.
02
Scoring Algorithm
Rule-based incident scoring: severity level, cascade count, chattering detection, trend direction, and urgency escalation. Transparent, debuggable, and ISA-18.2 aligned.
03
Role Engine + AI
Three role views (Operator, Engineer, Supervisor) with tailored guidance. Optional LLM narration converts structured incident JSON into plain-English summaries.
03B / Scoring Algorithm
